Positional encoding
SA width: ~1800km
SA Height: ~1200km

1. Suppose that South Africa is in euclidean space with the centre of South Africa at the origin. We want to find the centre of each province and use that as the mean for a 2d guassian from which the position of a lising will be sampled from, translate South Africa so that it lies in the positive quadrant and change to a basis with more variance along the width.

Getting the centres:
Consider the approximate width and height of south Africa, we treat the distances as pixels on the map, thus the pixel postion (900,600) is the centre of the map. We centre this at the origin. Since we want all the positional encoding vectors to be positive, we translate the map into the top-right quadrant. We translate the map centre by (900,600). The centres of each province were eyeballed. After the transformations, we get positive positional encoding which preserves approximate spatial distances between listings in different provinces.
